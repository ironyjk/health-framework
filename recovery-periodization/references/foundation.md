# Recovery & Periodization -- Background & Theory

> This document contains the theoretical foundations, evidence base, and references for this framework.
> For practical application and execution, see [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md).

## Evidence Strength

- SRA model: **Strong** (Zatsiorsky, Matveev, Fitness-Fatigue model)
- Benefits of deload cycles: **Moderate–Strong** (few direct RCTs, but consistent with coaching experience and advanced-athlete data)
- Correlation between HRV and training adaptation: **Moderate** (useful for within-individual trends, limited for absolute comparisons)
- Elevated resting heart rate = overwork signal: **Moderate**
- Physiological markers of overtraining syndrome: **Moderate–Mixed** (clinical diagnostic criteria remain loose)
- Claim that "cold plunge·sauna = accelerated recovery": **Weak–Mixed** (valid in specific contexts)


## Origins of Theory & Evidence

- **Hans Selye** — General Adaptation Syndrome (Alarm-Resistance-Exhaustion)
- **Yakovlev** — Supercompensation curve (simplified model, with limitations)
- **Zatsiorsky & Kraemer** — Fitness-Fatigue two-factor model (more accurate)
- **Matveev** — Periodization archetype (Classical Periodization)
- **Issurin** — Block Periodization
- **Tudor Bompa** — *Periodization: Theory and Methodology of Training*
- **Kellmann, Meeusen et al.** — Overreaching/Overtraining consensus (2013 Euro J Sport Sci)


## Core Concepts

### 1. SRA Curve
```
Stimulus → Fatigue (performance decrement) → Recovery (return to baseline) → Adaptation (above baseline)
```
Larger stimulus intensity means deeper Fatigue and longer Recovery. Ideally, the next stimulus should be applied during the **Adaptation upswing**.

### 2. Fitness-Fatigue Two-Factor Model
- Performance = Fitness - Fatigue
- Fitness: accumulates slowly, decays slowly
- Fatigue: accumulates quickly, decays quickly
- **Taper**: a few days of reduced training load → Fatigue↓ > Fitness↓ → peak performance

### 3. Forms of Periodization
- **Linear**: volume↓ · intensity↑ (classical blocks)
- **Undulating (DUP)**: intensity·reps fluctuate on a weekly/daily basis
- **Block (Issurin)**: 2–4 weeks accumulation → 1 week transformation → 1 week peaking, repeated
- **Conjugate (Westside)**: mixes max-effort, dynamic-effort, and repetition days

### 4. Deload
- After 4–6 weeks of accumulation, one week with volume -40~60%, intensity -10%
- Neural deload: preserve volume, intensity -20%
- More necessary for advanced trainees

### 5. Overreaching vs Overtraining
| State | Duration | Recovery | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Functional Overreaching | days–weeks | performance rebound within ~2 weeks | Intentional accumulation |
| Non-functional Overreaching | several weeks | weeks–months | Unintentional overload |
| **Overtraining Syndrome** | months+ | months–years | Broad endocrine·immune·psychological involvement |

### 6. Overtraining vs Burnout — Differentiation Flowchart

Athletic overtraining syndrome and occupational·psychological burnout (Maslach Burnout) have **extensively overlapping symptoms**. Persistent fatigue, disrupted sleep, low mood, and loss of motivation look identical in both. Misclassification leads to the wrong response (overtraining needs rest; burnout needs environmental·meaning redesign).

**Primary differentiation questions — if 2 of 3 are "yes," prioritize overtraining:**
- Training volume·intensity over the past 4–8 weeks has been ↑ vs baseline
- Performance decline is confirmed by objective measurement (1RM·power·time)
- Fatigue·performance decrement persists even during weeks fully disconnected from work (vacation·travel)

**Primary differentiation questions — if 2 of 3 are "yes," prioritize burnout:**
- Cynicism·distance·worthlessness toward work·relationships persists on a weekly basis
- Rapid recovery (within days) when disconnected from work over vacations·weekends
- Fatigue exists even though training is the same or reduced

**Shared territory (both apply):**
- HRV decline, RHR rise, sleep fragmentation — physiological markers appear similarly in both
- Frequent infections, slow wound healing
- Appetite·body weight changes
- In these cases, prioritize **allostatic load recalibration across life + specialist evaluation**

**Maslach Three Axes (burnout diagnostic aid):**
| Axis | Description | Difference from Overtraining |
|---|---|---|
| Exhaustion | Emotional·physical depletion | Overlaps with overtraining |
| Cynicism | Distance·cynicism toward work·people | **Unique to burnout** — rarely present in overtraining |
| Inefficacy | Loss of achievement·"I'm useless" | **Unique to burnout** — overtraining has performance decrement but less self-evaluation distortion |

**Differences in intervention direction:**
- Overtraining → reduce training volume 40–60%, deload 2–4 weeks, pause 1–3 months if needed. Solvable within this framework.
- Burnout → training reduction alone does not resolve it. Requires environmental redesign: work boundaries, meaning, control, peer support. Recommend the `counsel` skill or professional consultation. Maintain exercise but shift to "enjoyment-focused" (temporarily reduce high-intensity training).

**Misdiagnosis risk:** Prescribing only "rest more" to a burnout sufferer worsens isolation·mood decline. Attributing overtraining to "psychological issues" prolongs endocrine·immune damage.
