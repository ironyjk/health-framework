# Recovery & Periodization

> **Background and theory**: Read [references/foundation.md](references/foundation.md)


## One-Line Summary

**Adaptation happens during recovery, not training.** You must understand the Stimulus-Recovery-Adaptation (SRA) curve and use periodization to intentionally alternate accumulation and reduction phases in order to sustain long-term progression.


## Recovery Metrics

### Objective
- **HRV (heart rate variability)** — morning average, individual baseline trend. 7-day rolling vs 28-day
- **RHR (resting heart rate)** — sustained baseline +5bpm or more = signal
- **Body weight + appetite** — sharp declining trend
- **Sleep quality·efficiency**
- **Performance markers** — RPE·heart rate rising at the same workload

### Subjective
- **POMS·RESTQ-Sport** questionnaires
- Simple 1–10 rating of "Am I looking forward to training today?"
- Condition journal (3–5 min/day)

### Combined Warning Signs
- 4 consecutive sessions of RPE rising at the same weight
- RHR +7–10bpm sustained for 1 week+
- Worsening sleep + low mood
- Recurring minor injuries·infections


## Practical Application

### Intermediate Resistance Training (5/3/1 cycle)
```
W1: 5/5/5+ @65/75/85%
W2: 3/3/3+ @70/80/90%
W3: 5/3/1+ @75/85/95%
W4: Deload 5/5/5 @40/50/60%
```

### Hypertrophy Block (RP)
```
4–6 weeks accumulation (MEV → MRV)
1 week deload (volume 50%, maintain intensity)
Repeat or transition to intensity block
```

### Endurance Periodization
```
Base 8–12 weeks (Z2 ~80% of the week)
Build 4–8 weeks (VO2 ↑, threshold)
Peak·Taper 1–3 weeks
Race
Transition 2–4 weeks (recovery, low volume)
```

### Daily Recovery Tools
- **Sleep**: The most powerful. See `sleep-foundations`
- **Sufficient protein·carbs**: `macro-tracking`
- **Stress management**: meditation·mindfulness (moderate evidence)
- **Light aerobic (Z1) next day**: active recovery, promotes blood flow
- **Sauna**: heat acclimation·cardiovascular benefits (moderate evidence, Laukkanen 2015), weak evidence for recovery acceleration
- **Cold plunge**: may **impair hypertrophy** immediately after strength training (Roberts 2015). Neutral–beneficial possible for endurance. Effective for maintaining performance between tournaments·consecutive game days
- **Massage·foam rolling**: subjective recovery·reduced pain perception (ROM effects limited)

### Adjusting Recovery to the Female Menstrual Cycle

- **Follicular phase (menstruation start ~ ovulation)**: rising estrogen — relatively superior recovery capacity, favorable for PRs·high volume
- **Ovulation ~ early luteal**: stable
- **Luteal phase (5–7 days before menstruation)**: elevated body temperature·heart rate·sleep fragmentation. Performance may drop ±5–10% at the same RPE. Maintain volume but allow RIR cushion / shift deload to weeks with high stress
- **Menstruation**: subjective discomfort·iron loss. Favor light activity, prioritize individual response
- **RED-S risk**: amenorrhea 3 months+ + energy availability <30 kcal/kg FFM = stop training and seek medical evaluation
- Data: apps such as FitrWoman·Clue + cross-reference with training log

### RED-S Checklist (Based on IOC 2018)

If ≥3 of the following apply, suspect RED-S and seek specialist evaluation:

- Amenorrhea 3 months+ or irregular menstrual cycle
- Sustained decline in body weight·body fat, or low body weight (BMI <18.5)
- Performance decline (identical training feels markedly harder than before)
- Recurring stress fractures·overuse injuries
- Frequent infections·colds
- Low mood·reduced concentration·worsening sleep
- Unusually low resting heart rate (overtraining-related)·Raynaud's symptoms
- Dietary restriction·signs of body-image obsession
- Constipation·reduced bowel function
- **Also occurs in men** — signs of low morning testosterone·reduced libido

### Non-training allostatic load (cumulative)

The adaptation budget is not spent on training alone. Manage the following cumulatively:

- **Work stress** (deadlines·overtime·conflicts)
- **Family** (young children·caregiving·relationship crises)
- **48–72h after infections·vaccinations**
- **Jet lag·business travel**
- **Noisy·polluted environments**
- **Financial·legal stress**

In weeks with high total load, preemptively adjust training volume -20~30% (not in reactive response).


## Antipatterns

- **Long-term skipping of deload** — fear of "getting weaker," actually leads to regression
- **Adding more volume in response to fatigue·performance decline** — the most common failure
- **Reacting to a single day's HRV value** — the indicator is weekly/week-over-week trend
- **Over-reliance on a single tool** (ice baths·sauna·supplements) — sleep·nutrition come first
- **Unprincipled "rest when mood is bad"** — unstable training adaptation
- **Leaving fatigue in and under-tapering before competition** — performance decline
- **Ignoring non-training stress (work·family·sleep·infection)** — total allostatic load is cumulative
- **Linear "more = better" thinking**


## Limitations

1. **Limits of the simplified supercompensation model** — actual adaptation spans multiple timescales
2. **Difficulty interpreting HRV** — sensitive to morning·standing·cycle·breathing. Device variation
3. **Loose diagnostic criteria for overtraining** — diagnosis by exclusion, takes a long time
4. **Individual differences** — the same program may be insufficient for one person, excessive for another
5. **Life factors outside training** are major variables — uncontrollable domains exist
6. **Marketing excess for recovery tools** — few gadgets deliver effects comparable to the fundamentals (sleep·nutrition)
