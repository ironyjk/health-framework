# Hypertrophy Volume Management

> **Background and theory**: Read [references/foundation.md](references/foundation.md)


## One-Line Summary

**The primary variable for hypertrophy is volume (weekly sets per muscle group taken close to failure).** The 5–30 rep range is largely equivalent for hypertrophy. The key is to **find your MEV, MAV, and MRV zones and oscillate between them over time**.


## When to Use

- Goals of **hypertrophy and body recomposition**
- After building a strength base (6–12+ months)
- Visual goals (bodybuilding, competition, physique)
- Needing localized stimulus post-injury or rehab
- Designing accessory work for a strength program


## Representative Splits

### PPL (Push/Pull/Legs) — 6 days/week, advanced
```
Day 1 Push: Bench, Incline, Shoulder Press, Lateral Raise, Dips
Day 2 Pull: Deadlift, Pull-up, Barbell Row, Face Pull, Curls
Day 3 Legs: Squat, RDL, Leg Press, Calves, Hip Thrust
Repeat 2× + 1 rest day
```

### Upper/Lower — 4 days/week (intermediate)
```
Mon Upper / Tue Lower / Thu Upper / Fri Lower
1–2 compounds + 2–3 accessories per muscle group
```

### Full Body — 3 days/week (intermediate, time-constrained)
```
2 compounds + hit weekly MEV per muscle group
```

### Arnold Split / Bro Split (1×/week per muscle) — less recommended
- Volume ceiling and recovery inefficiency, though advanced lifters may prefer it


## Exercise Selection Principles

- 2–3 exercises per muscle group targeting **different angles/lengths**
- Prioritize exercises that **load the stretched position** (recent literature)
  - Pectorals → Incline Dumbbell, Pec Deck
  - Hamstrings → RDL, Seated Leg Curl
  - Quadriceps → High-bar Squat, Hack Squat
  - Biceps → Incline Curl
- Build a foundation with compounds, fine-tune with single-joint work


## Practical Checklist

- Training log (sets, weight, reps, RIR)
- Increase weekly sets by +2–4 every 4–6 week block → approach MRV → deload
- Subjective markers: soreness lasting 2–3 days, performance decline, disturbed sleep = MRV warning


## Anti-Patterns

- **Chasing total volume alone**, ignoring RIR intensity → "junk volume"
- **Every set to failure every week** → CNS drain, reaching MRV too early
- **Muscle imbalance** — overemphasis on mirror muscles (chest, biceps, abs) and neglect of back, hamstrings
- **Rep range tunnel vision** — only low or only high reps
- **Neglecting nutrition** — high volume without a surplus and 1.6–2.0 g/kg protein during a bulk leads to muscle loss
- **Chasing hormonal "hacks"** — natural hypertrophy has an upper bound (Helms' maximum adaptable potential)
- **"Pump = growth" myth** — transient blood flow and water. Don't equate with growth signaling
- **Fixed 30-second rests** — 2–3 min for compounds and 1–2 min for single-joint work is better for volume and performance


## Application to Women, Older Adults, GLP-1 Users

- **Women**: volume response is similar to men, though absolute gains in lean mass are smaller (hormones). Hypertrophy principles are identical. Lower-body responsiveness tends to be relatively stronger than in men (general tendency)
- **Women's menstrual cycle (optional adjustment)**: follicular phase favors PRs and high-volume work; luteal phase may benefit from RPE slack (RIR +1) or shifting to accessories. Not a hard rule
- **Postmenopausal women**: resistance training becomes more important (bone density, sarcopenia). Maintain volume, extend recovery time
- **Older adults (65+)**: start MEV low (6–8 sets/week), intensity 70–80% 1RM, RIR 2–3. 2–3× per week full body. Machines and dumbbells acceptable
- **GLP-1 users**: to preserve LBM — maintain MEV volume with 1.2–1.6 g/kg LBM protein. High-volume bulking blocks are unrealistic given suppressed recovery and appetite


## Realities of Korean Gyms

- **Availability of heavy compounds** varies — 24-hour gyms and apartment gyms often cap dumbbells at 30 kg. Alternatives: machines, high reps, unilateral work, pause reps
- **Rush hours** (19:00–22:00) make high-volume PPL or 4-way splits hard to execute → compress with circuits and supersets
- **If working with a PT**: prioritize program consistency. A "fitness show" that switches body parts every PT session won't even hit MEV


## Limitations

1. **Inefficient for absolute strength and sprinting** — complement with `strength-basics`
2. **High time cost** — 4–6 days/week, 60–90 min sessions
3. **Large individual variation in MEV/MRV** — copying someone else's chart will be inaccurate
4. **Genetic ceiling exists** — responder variance
5. **Little direct contribution to endurance or cardiovascular adaptation**
6. **Overstated claims from bodybuilding culture** — detox shakes, mTOR windows, and similar are mostly tertiary variables or below
