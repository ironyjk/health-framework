# Hypertrophy Volume Management -- Background & Theory

> This document contains the theoretical foundations, evidence base, and references for this framework.
> For practical application and execution, see [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md).

## Evidence Strength

- Weekly sets and hypertrophy dose-response: **Strong** (Schoenfeld & Krieger meta-analyses)
- Equivalence of 5–30 rep range: **Moderate–Strong** (conditional on proximity to failure)
- Importance of proximity to failure per set (RIR 0–3): **Strong**
- Frequency ≥2×/week/muscle > 1×: **Moderate** (small difference when volume is equated)
- Specific "hypertrophy-only" techniques (drop sets, rest-pause): **Moderate** (time-efficiency advantage; unclear whether they exceed total volume)


## Origin of Theory & Evidence

- **Brad Schoenfeld** — numerous hypertrophy meta-analyses. *Science and Development of Muscle Hypertrophy*
- **James Krieger** — set-response meta-analyses
- **Mike Israetel & Renaissance Periodization (RP)** — popularized the MV/MEV/MAV/MRV model

## Core Concepts

### 1. Volume Units
- **Hard Set** = a set taken to within 0–3 reps of failure (RIR 0–3)
- Hypertrophy volume is measured as weekly hard sets per muscle group

### 2. Volume Zones (RP Model)
- **MV (Maintenance Volume)**: minimum to maintain muscle mass
- **MEV (Minimum Effective Volume)**: minimum for growth to begin. Roughly 8–10 sets per week per muscle group
- **MAV (Maximum Adaptive Volume)**: zone of maximal gains. 12–20 sets per week per muscle group
- **MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume)**: sustained excess leads to accumulated overload

| Muscle Group | MV | MEV | MAV | MRV |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pectorals | 4 | 8 | 12–20 | 22 |
| Lats/Back | 6 | 10 | 14–22 | 25 |
| Quadriceps | 6 | 8 | 12–18 | 20 |
| Hamstrings | 3 | 6 | 10–16 | 18 |
| Glutes | 0 | 0 | 4–12 | 16 |
| Side Delts | 0 | 8 | 16–22 | 26 |
| Triceps | 4 | 6 | 10–14 | 18 |
| Biceps | 5 | 8 | 12–20 | 22 |

Individual variation ±30%. Confirm your own MEV/MRV by actual response.

### 3. Rep Ranges
- **5–10 reps**: good for load and time-efficiency
- **10–20 reps**: strong local muscular fatigue signal
- **20–30 reps**: low joint stress, also delivers conditioning effects
- **Conclusion**: hypertrophy is equivalent as long as training is close to failure. Allocate by exercise and joint considerations

### 4. Frequency
- **2–3× per week per muscle** is generally superior to 1×/week (small difference with volume equated, but distribution aids recovery)

### 5. Programming Blocks (RP-style)
```
Block 1 (Accumulation): MEV → MRV, 4–6 weeks
Deload 1 week
Block 2 (Intensification): intensity rises, volume drops
Deload
```
